EXAMPLE SAR — LAYERING VIA HIGH-RISK JURISDICTION
Type: Layering | Profile: Salaried Employee | Jurisdiction: UAE
---
The filing institution is submitting this Suspicious Activity Report concerning a current account held by a salaried employee at this institution. The account was flagged by the institution's automated transaction monitoring system following the detection of anomalous inbound and outbound transfer activity during a three-day monitoring period. The filing institution has conducted a preliminary review of the account activity and determined that the observed pattern meets the threshold for SAR submission under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA) 2002, Section 12.

During the monitoring period, the account received twenty-eight inbound fund transfers originating from multiple domestic sources, with an aggregate value of INR 14,50,000. The transfers ranged in individual value and were received across the three-day window in rapid succession. Within hours of each inbound receipt, the account holder initiated corresponding outbound international wire transfers directing the consolidated funds to beneficiary accounts held at financial institutions domiciled in the United Arab Emirates, a jurisdiction designated as high-risk by the Financial Action Task Force. The residual account balance returned to near zero following each completed outbound cycle, indicating that the account was being used as a pass-through conduit rather than for legitimate accumulation or expenditure.

The volume and velocity of the observed transactions represent a marked and statistically significant deviation from the account holder's established financial baseline. The account holder's declared monthly income and documented twelve-month average monthly deposits are inconsistent with the total value transferred during the monitoring period, representing a deviation of 3,715.8 percent above the historical baseline. The average individual transaction amount of INR 51,786 fell consistently below the RBI reporting threshold of INR 1,00,000, a pattern characteristic of deliberate structuring to avoid mandatory currency transaction reporting obligations under RBI Master Direction 2023.

The observed fund flow pattern is consistent with the recognised AML typology of layering, specifically the sub-typology of pass-through account usage combined with rapid cross-border fund movement. The involvement of a FATF-designated high-risk jurisdiction as the sole destination for outbound transfers, combined with the speed of fund movement and the sub-threshold individual transaction sizing, is indicative of a coordinated effort to introduce illicit funds into the financial system, obscure their origin through layered cross-border transactions, and defeat automated audit trail mechanisms. The following AML rules were triggered: Structuring and Smurfing (AML-001), High Velocity Transactions (AML-002), Profile Inconsistency (AML-005), High Risk Jurisdiction Transfer (AML-006), and Rapid Fund Movement and Layering (AML-007).

Based on the totality of evidence assembled during this review, the filing institution has determined that the transaction activity is suspicious and does not have a discernible lawful explanation consistent with the account holder's declared profile. This SAR is filed pursuant to PMLA Section 12 and Rule 3 of the Prevention of Money Laundering (Maintenance of Records) Rules 2005. The account has been placed under enhanced transaction monitoring with immediate effect. All related accounts identified through network analysis have been flagged for review, and the matter has been escalated to the institution's Financial Intelligence Unit for further investigation.

EXAMPLE SAR — STRUCTURING AND SMURFING
Type: Structuring | Profile: Retail Business Owner | Jurisdiction: Domestic
---
The filing institution submits this Suspicious Activity Report in respect of a current account held in the name of a registered retail business entity at this institution. The account was identified through the institution's rule-based transaction surveillance system as exhibiting indicators consistent with deliberate structuring of cash deposits to circumvent mandatory reporting thresholds established by the Reserve Bank of India. The review period encompasses fourteen consecutive calendar days during which anomalous deposit activity was recorded.

During the fourteen-day monitoring period, the account received forty-two separate cash deposit transactions with an aggregate deposited value of INR 38,50,000. Each individual deposit was sized between INR 65,000 and INR 95,000, placing every transaction within the seventy to ninety-nine percent band of the RBI mandatory Currency Transaction Report threshold of INR 1,00,000. No single transaction met or exceeded the reporting threshold. The deposits were made across nine different branch locations and two separate ATM deposit channels associated with this institution, indicating deliberate geographic dispersal to reduce the visibility of the aggregated deposit pattern to any single branch officer.

The transaction pattern deviates substantially from the account's established activity profile. Over the preceding twelve months, the account averaged eight deposit transactions per month with an average monthly deposit value of INR 2,20,000. The volume observed during the monitoring period represents a 525 percent increase in transaction count and a 645 percent increase in deposited value relative to the documented monthly baseline. The declared annual turnover of the registered business entity is inconsistent with a deposit concentration of this magnitude within a fourteen-day window.

The deposit pattern is consistent with the AML typology of structuring, also referred to as smurfing, wherein a larger sum of funds is deliberately divided into multiple smaller transactions each designed to fall below mandatory reporting thresholds. The systematic sub-threshold sizing across forty-two transactions, combined with the deliberate use of multiple deposit channels and branch locations, demonstrates a level of operational planning that is inconsistent with routine business cash management. The triggered compliance rules include Structuring and Smurfing (AML-001), Below Threshold Structuring (AML-003), Large Value Transaction in aggregate (AML-004), and Confirmed Structuring Pattern (AML-012).

The filing institution has determined that the observed deposit activity is suspicious and lacks a plausible legitimate business explanation proportionate to the account holder's declared profile and sector. This report is filed pursuant to PMLA Section 12. The account has been referred for enhanced due diligence review including re-verification of beneficial ownership and source of funds documentation. A cash transaction freeze has been applied pending the outcome of the internal investigation, and the case has been referred to the institution's Senior Compliance Officer for escalation to the Financial Intelligence Unit of India.

EXAMPLE SAR — ROUND TRIPPING AND CROSS-BORDER LAYERING
Type: Round Tripping | Profile: Import-Export Business | Jurisdiction: Mauritius, Cayman Islands
---
The filing institution files this Suspicious Activity Report in connection with a current account held by an import-export trading entity registered under the Companies Act. The account was identified by the institution's enhanced monitoring programme following the detection of circular fund flow patterns involving multiple offshore jurisdictions. The monitoring period under review spans twenty-one calendar days.

During the monitoring period, the account received four inbound international wire transfers originating from corporate entities domiciled in Mauritius, aggregating to INR 92,00,000. Within three to five business days of each inbound receipt, the account executed outbound wire transfers of equivalent or near-equivalent value directed to separate corporate entities domiciled in the Cayman Islands. The net change in account balance across the full monitoring period was negligible, with the closing balance differing from the opening balance by less than INR 50,000, confirming that the account was used exclusively as a transit mechanism with no apparent commercial utility retained by the account holder.

The transaction volumes observed during the monitoring period bear no relationship to the entity's declared import-export business activity. The entity's audited financial statements for the preceding fiscal year reported gross revenues of INR 1,80,00,000. The funds transited through the account during the twenty-one-day monitoring period represent approximately fifty-one percent of the entity's declared annual revenue concentrated within a three-week window, with no corresponding documentation of goods, services, or contractual obligations that would provide a legitimate trade finance explanation for the fund flows.

The circular fund flow pattern — offshore inflows followed by equivalent offshore outflows through a domestic transit account, with no residual value and no identifiable commercial purpose — is consistent with the recognised AML typology of round tripping. This typology is commonly employed to repatriate funds of illicit origin as apparent legitimate foreign investment income, thereby integrating proceeds of crime into the formal financial system with an apparent regulatory-compliant audit trail. Both Mauritius and the Cayman Islands appear on the institution's enhanced jurisdiction monitoring list. The triggered rules include Round Tripping (AML-009), High Risk Jurisdiction Transfer (AML-006), Rapid Fund Movement and Layering (AML-007), Large Value Transaction (AML-004), and Multi Account Layering (AML-011).

The filing institution has determined that the transaction activity constitutes suspicious activity under PMLA Section 12 and does not have a credible legitimate trade or investment explanation consistent with the entity's declared commercial profile. The account has been suspended pending further investigation. Source of funds documentation, contractual agreements, and beneficial ownership declarations have been requested from the account holder. The matter has been escalated to the institution's Financial Intelligence Unit and external legal counsel has been notified.

EXAMPLE SAR — RAPID FUND MOVEMENT, SAVINGS ACCOUNT MISUSE
Type: Rapid Fund Movement | Profile: Student | Jurisdiction: UAE
---
The filing institution submits this Suspicious Activity Report in respect of a savings account held in the name of an individual declared as a student at the time of account opening. The account was flagged by the institution's monitoring system following detection of transaction activity materially inconsistent with the account holder's declared occupational and financial profile. The monitoring period spans seven consecutive calendar days.

During the seven-day monitoring period, the account received nineteen inbound fund transfers from sixteen distinct domestic remitters, with an aggregate received value of INR 9,50,000. Within twenty-four to forty-eight hours of each inbound receipt, the account holder initiated outbound international wire transfers directed to beneficiary accounts held at financial institutions in the United Arab Emirates. The outbound transfers closely mirrored the inbound amounts, with the account balance returning to a nominal level following each transfer cycle. The use of sixteen distinct remitters is consistent with the use of a network of associates to aggregate funds prior to cross-border transfer, a technique associated with organised layering operations.

The transaction activity is entirely inconsistent with the account holder's declared student profile and the expected usage pattern for a savings account of this classification. The account holder's declared financial profile would suggest a maximum expected monthly transaction volume of INR 50,000 to INR 1,00,000 based on institutional benchmarks for the student customer segment. The INR 9,50,000 transited during the seven-day monitoring period represents a deviation of approximately 850 to 1,900 percent above the expected monthly profile ceiling. The average individual transaction amount of INR 50,000 falls below the RBI CTR reporting threshold, consistent with deliberate below-threshold transaction sizing.

The pattern of multiple inbound transfers from dispersed domestic sources followed by rapid outbound cross-border consolidation is consistent with the AML typology of layering with a pass-through mechanism, combined with indicators of smurfing at the inbound stage. The use of a student savings account for high-volume cross-border fund movement, the involvement of a FATF high-risk jurisdiction, the sub-threshold individual transaction sizing, and the rapid turnaround time between receipt and remittance collectively represent strong indicators of money laundering activity. Triggered rules include Profile Inconsistency (AML-005), High Risk Jurisdiction Transfer (AML-006), Rapid Fund Movement and Layering (AML-007), Structuring and Smurfing (AML-001), Account Type Mismatch (AML-013), and High Velocity Transactions (AML-002).

The filing institution has determined that the account activity is suspicious and files this SAR under PMLA Section 12. The account has been placed under an immediate transaction block pending completion of enhanced due diligence. Source of funds enquiries have been initiated with each identified inbound remitter. The account holder has been requested to provide documentary evidence of the commercial basis for the observed transactions. The matter has been escalated to the institution's Senior AML Officer.

EXAMPLE SAR — CASH INTENSIVE ACTIVITY, MULTIPLE DEPOSITS
Type: Cash Intensive | Profile: Salaried Employee | Jurisdiction: Domestic
---
The filing institution files this Suspicious Activity Report in connection with a savings account held by an individual whose declared occupation is salaried employment in the private sector. The account was identified through the institution's monitoring system as exhibiting cash deposit patterns inconsistent with the account holder's declared employment income and occupational profile. The monitoring period under review spans thirty calendar days.

During the thirty-day monitoring period, the account received thirty-one separate over-the-counter cash deposits with an aggregate value of INR 24,80,000. The deposits were made on twenty-six of the thirty calendar days in the monitoring period, indicating a near-daily deposit cadence. Individual deposit amounts ranged between INR 40,000 and INR 98,000, with no single deposit meeting or exceeding the RBI mandatory CTR threshold of INR 1,00,000. The consistent sub-threshold sizing across thirty-one transactions is statistically improbable as a result of natural variation in legitimate cash receipts and is consistent with deliberate threshold avoidance.

The observed cash deposit volume is irreconcilable with the account holder's declared monthly salary of INR 45,000 per month. The INR 24,80,000 deposited during the thirty-day monitoring period represents approximately fifty-five months of the account holder's declared gross income concentrated within a single calendar month. No supplemental income sources have been declared or documented on the account holder's KYC record. The account holder's declared occupation does not involve cash-intensive commercial activity that would provide a legitimate explanation for this volume of cash receipts.

The pattern of frequent, near-daily cash deposits consistently sized below the mandatory reporting threshold across multiple calendar days is consistent with the AML typology of cash placement with deliberate structuring. This typology represents the placement stage of the money laundering cycle, wherein illicit cash proceeds are introduced into the formal financial system through repeated sub-threshold deposits designed to aggregate funds without triggering mandatory reporting obligations. The triggered compliance rules include Structuring and Smurfing (AML-001), Below Threshold Structuring (AML-003), Cash Intensive Activity (AML-010), Large Value Transaction in aggregate (AML-004), and Profile Inconsistency (AML-005).

The filing institution has determined that the cash deposit pattern is suspicious and files this SAR pursuant to PMLA Section 12 and the RBI Master Direction on KYC 2016. The account has been placed under enhanced monitoring and cash deposit restrictions have been applied pending completion of a source of funds review. The account holder has been contacted and requested to provide documentary evidence of the source of the cash deposited. The matter has been escalated internally to the institution's Financial Intelligence Unit.
